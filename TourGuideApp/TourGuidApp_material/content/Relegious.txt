
1-Mosque of Abu al-Abbas al-Mursi

overview
 It is dedicated to the 13th century Murcian Andalusi Sufi saint Abul Abbas al-Mursi, whose tomb it contains.
address
 Anfoushi neighborhood of Alexandria, near the Citadel of Qaitbay.

2-Saint Mark's Coptic Orthodox Cathedral

The former seat of the Coptic Orthodox Patriarch prior to being moved to Cairo in 1968. This Cathedral
 is an excellent example of Coptic architecture and is a wonder to behold. 

Address:
19 Qesm Al Attarin, Alexandria EgyptMap

4-El Qaed Ibrahim Mosque

overview
one of the most famous mosques built in Alexandria in the area of The Sand Station,in Egypt, is famous for its long and graceful minaret and also attracting worshippers from all the neighborhoods of Alexandria, especially in the month of Ramadan. Its construction dates back to 1948 and was designed by Egyptian-based Italian architect Mario Rossi. address
Mahtet El Raml Square, Alexandria 21599 Egypt
